Department Vision & Mission

Vision

To evolve as a leading hub for **Computer Science Engineering** specializing in **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning**, producing globally competitive professionals and pioneering research for societal needs.

Mission

  1. To impart rigorous, research-oriented education in core AI, ML, and Data Science concepts.
  2. To establish state-of-the-art laboratory facilities and foster collaborations with industry.
  3. To cultivate strong ethical values, leadership, and lifelong learning attitudes in students.

Program Outcomes (POs) & Educational Objectives (PEOs)

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

  1. **PEO 1: Core Competency:** To enable graduates to apply expertise in AI and Machine Learning to solve real-world engineering problems in industry or pursue advanced studies.
  2. **PEO 2: Professional Development:** To encourage graduates to demonstrate strong communication, teamwork, and leadership skills in diverse, ethical, and professional environments.
  3. **PEO 3: Lifelong Learning:** To inspire graduates to engage in continuous professional development and adapt to rapidly evolving technological advancements in computing.

Program Outcomes (POs)

  • **PO1 Engineering knowledge:** Apply knowledge of mathematics, science, engineering fundamentals, and an engineering specialization to solve complex engineering problems.
  • **PO2 Problem analysis:** Identify, formulate, review research literature, and analyze complex engineering problems reaching substantiated conclusions.
  • **PO3 Design/development of solutions:** Design solutions for complex engineering problems and design system components or processes that meet specified needs.
  • **PO4 Conduct investigations of complex problems:** Use research-based knowledge and methods including design of experiments, analysis and interpretation of data, and synthesis of the information to provide valid conclusions.
  • **PO5 Modern tool usage:** Create, select, and apply appropriate techniques, resources, and modern engineering and IT tools, including prediction and modeling, to complex engineering activities.
  • **PO6 The engineer and society:** Apply reasoning informed by contextual knowledge to assess societal, health, safety, legal, and cultural issues and the consequent responsibilities relevant to professional engineering practice.
  • **PO7 Environment and sustainability:** Understand the impact of professional engineering solutions in societal and environmental contexts.
  • **PO8 Ethics:** Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities and norms of the engineering practice.
  • **PO9 Individual and teamwork:** Function effectively as an individual, and as a member or leader in diverse teams, and in multidisciplinary settings.
  • **PO10 Communication:** Communicate effectively on complex engineering activities with the engineering community and with society at large.
  • **PO11 Project management and finance:** Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of engineering and management principles and apply these to one‚Äôs own work, as a member and leader in a team, to manage projects and in multidisciplinary environments.
  • **PO12 Lifelong learning:** Recognize the need for, and have the preparation and ability to engage in independent and life-long learning in the broadest context of technological change.
